What is NEXUS

A scientific instrument, not a product

NEXUS is a PhD research platform for studying how macroeconomic phenomena emerge from the collision of millions of heterogeneous decisions. It is not a trading system, not a price predictor, and not a chatbot that reads news.

The central scientific question it attacks is the Meese-Rogoff Exchange Rate Disconnect Puzzle — first documented in 1983, still unresolved: why do macro fundamentals fail to predict short-run exchange rates, yet dominate in the long run?

The hypothesis is that the answer lies in agent heterogeneity: hedge funds process a Fed announcement in 2 minutes. Households in Naples process it 6 weeks later through their electricity bill. That time-gap between tiers generates the disconnect. NEXUS makes this mechanism visible and measurable.

Open puzzle — 40+ years unsolved
"Empirical exchange rate models do not outperform a random walk at any horizon up to one year."

The twin earth population

Seven tiers. One world.

Every agent has a distinct information set, time horizon, cognitive architecture, and objective function. They are calibrated from real data sources — not hand-tuned. The same macro shock hits all seven tiers simultaneously, but each tier processes it differently and at a different speed.

T1 · Sovereign
Central banks
Fed, ECB, BoJ, BoE, SNB, PBoC. Set policy rates, conduct FX interventions, issue forward guidance. The macro event generators.
SpeedQuarterly + continuous
Data sourceFOMC transcripts, Taylor rule
T2 · Institutional
Global macro hedge funds
Bridgewater, Brevan Howard-type agents. Carry trades, momentum, macro fundamentals. Primary channel of fundamental price discovery.
SpeedMinutes to days
Data sourceCFTC CoT, 13F filings
T3 · Intermediary
Commercial & investment banks
FX market makers. See aggregate client order flow before it hits the market — the private information advantage that creates short-run price pressure.
SpeedSeconds to minutes
Data sourceBIS triennial FX survey
T4 · Institutional
Institutional asset managers
Pension funds, SWFs, insurance companies. Mechanical FX rebalancing when asset prices move. Slow but enormous — billions in systematic flows.
SpeedWeekly to monthly
Data sourcePension FX hedge surveys
T5 · Professional
Professional retail FX traders
Technical analysis, economic calendars, macro commentary. High enough literacy to move markets at the margin, wrong often enough to lose systematically.
SpeedMinutes to hours
Data sourceOANDA/IG positioning data
T6 · Retail
Ordinary retail FX traders
Social media-driven, FOMO, loss-averse. Scientifically important: they provide the irrational component that allows persistent disconnect from fundamentals.
SpeedHours to days
Data sourceESMA retail loss statistics
T7 · Household
Households & real economy
Never trade FX directly, but their savings, import consumption, and dollarization decisions ARE the macro fundamentals that eventually win. Closes the loop.
SpeedWeeks to months
Data sourceECB HFCS, payment data
Live scenario trace

Fed raises real rate +75bps

A single macro event propagates through all seven tiers over twelve weeks. This is the core scientific output — not a price forecast, but a mechanistic trace of how each tier amplifies, delays, or corrects the FX response.

Macro shock injected · t = 0
Federal Reserve raises federal funds rate by 75bps. Forward guidance signals two additional hikes. FX intervention desk activated.
Hedge funds
t = 2 min
Carry unwind. Short JPY/USD positions liquidated instantly. Broad USD buying begins. Price: USD +0.8% in first five minutes. Long-USD momentum positions accumulate.
Bank FX desks
t = 3–10 min
Spread widening. Bid-ask spreads widen to manage inventory risk. Client flow floods in — all USD buys. Banks capture spread, then accommodate flow. USD +1.3%.
Asset managers
t = 1–3 days
Mechanical rebalancing. Rate hike triggers bond price drop. Portfolio allocation shifts. FX hedges require systematic USD buying — ~$40B over 3 days. Amplifies initial move.
Pro retail
t = 30 min–2 days
Trend chasing. USD breaks resistance levels on charts. Long USD entries accumulate. Social media fills with bullish commentary. Noise increases.
Retail FX
t = 1–5 days
FOMO and panic. Half pile into USD long on headlines. Others short USD because "it moved too much." Retail losses increase. Price dispersion widens.
Households
t = 2–12 wks
The fundamental response begins. Higher US rates attract European household savings. Exporters revise hedging. Import costs rise, real consumption adjusts. This is what eventually corrects the overshoot — the disconnect closes from the bottom up.
Research branches

Six connected modules

All modules share the same synthetic population and agent infrastructure. Each one generates one PhD paper. Together they form a complete theory of how macro shocks propagate through every layer of the financial system.

M1 · MACRO
Macroeconomics & policy
How policy transmission works when agents have heterogeneous priors, different information sets, and update at different speeds.
  • Does forward guidance anchor expectations heterogeneously?
  • Is the fiscal multiplier a function of wealth distribution?
M2 · BANKING
Banking & systemic risk
Bank run dynamics, interbank contagion, and credit cycles as emergent properties of individual bank and household decisions.
  • Can SVB bank run dynamics be replicated from household social media reaction?
  • What capital ratio threshold prevents contagion?
M3 · FOREX
Foreign exchange markets
The core module. Exchange rate disconnect, overshooting, and fundamental convergence emerge from the seven-tier agent hierarchy.
  • Can heterogeneous information processing resolve Meese-Rogoff?
  • Which tier drives the overshoot and which corrects it?
M4 · CRYPTO
Crypto & DeFi
Crypto adoption as household portfolio substitution. Stablecoin depegging, liquidity crises, and Bitcoin as EM safe-haven asset.
  • Does crypto adoption follow epidemic contagion dynamics?
  • Can a stablecoin depeg trigger retail bank withdrawal cascades?
M5 · MANAGEMENT
Business & organizations
Firms as employers within the household population. Credit tightening, hiring, firm survival, and market concentration as emergent phenomena.
  • How do credit crunches cascade into small business failures?
  • Does market concentration emerge from repeated recessions?
M6 · HOUSEHOLD
Household financial behavior
The backbone layer. Income heterogeneity, financial literacy, dollarization in EM households, and the real-economy feedback loop.
  • Does financial literacy level predict crisis amplification?
  • When does EM household fear tip into currency crisis?
